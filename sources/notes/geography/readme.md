## Geography

### Earth atmosphere

Earth's atmosphere is about 300 miles (480 kilometers) thick, but most of it is within 10 miles (16 km) the surface. Air pressure decreases with altitude. At sea level, air pressure is about 14.7 pounds per square inch (1 kilogram per square centimeter). At 10,000 feet (3 km), the air pressure is 10 pounds per square inch (0.7 kg per square cm).

    Exosphere: 700 to 10,000 km (440 to 6,200 miles)
    Thermosphere: 80 to 700 km (50 to 440 miles)[15]
    Mesosphere: 50 to 80 km (31 to 50 miles)
    Stratosphere: 12 to 50 km (7 to 31 miles)
    Troposphere: 0 to 12 km (0 to 7 miles)[16]

    Nitrogen — 78 percent
    Oxygen — 21 percent
    Argon — 0.93 percent
    Carbon dioxide — 0.04 percent
